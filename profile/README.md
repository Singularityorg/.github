## рҹҢҢ What is Singularity?

**Singularity** is a free, open, Wikipedia-style learning platform where anyone in the world can learn how to do *anything* вҖ” from wiring an electrical circuit to building a distributed software system вҖ” without paywalls, gatekeepers, or fragmented, proprietary knowledge.

We are the operational home of the **B.L.U.E. System**, a concept 14 years in the making, envisioned by **Sebastian Rey**. Singularity exists to take that vision off a whiteboard and turn it into a living, breathing platform.

> *"Someone with the resources will eventually build this. Profit should not be the goal."*  
> вҖ” Sebastian Rey, creator of the B.L.U.E. System

The world's knowledge belongs to the world. This organization is how we give it back.

**How it works in practice:**

- Every guide begins at **Level 1** вҖ” no assumptions, no hidden prerequisites.
- Each level builds on the previous, forming a continuous tutorial from concept to capability.
- Guides at higher levels link *back* to the lower-level pages they depend on вҖ” so no learner is ever stranded.
- Complex real-world tasks (building a server, repairing an engine, growing food) are broken into levels, making them approachable for *anyone*.

This is the difference between Singularity and a traditional wiki: we don't just tell you *what* something is вҖ” we show you *how to do it*, step by step, from the ground up.

**Who are Verifiers?**

Verifiers are community members who have been **vetted and credentialed** in a specific domain. They are not corporate employees or paid gatekeepers вҖ” they are practitioners, experts, and experienced contributors who have earned trust within the community.

**How verification works:**

- Submitted content is assigned to a **jury of Verifiers** relevant to its domain.
- Verifiers independently review the content for accuracy, clarity, and appropriate level placement.
- A vote determines whether the content is approved, returned for revision, or rejected.
- No single Verifier has unilateral power вҖ” the jury model prevents individual bias from distorting the platform.

**Becoming a Verifier:**

Verifiers are identified through demonstrated expertise вҖ” contributions, credentials, or community reputation вҖ” and onboarded through a transparent vetting process documented in [`singularity/governance`](https://github.com/singularity-org/governance).

**The principle:**

When a guide instructs a learner to use a specific material, tool, or resource, a **contextual link to a relevant supplier** appears at exactly the point it is needed вҖ” not as a banner ad, not as a pop-up, not as a sponsored result.

**Why this is ethical:**

- Ads are **useful**, not disruptive. They appear because the learner *needs* the item right now.
- No personal data is harvested or sold to advertisers. Context comes from the *content*, not the *user*.
- Revenue is tied directly to the educational mission вҖ” more guides = more useful contextual links = sustainable funding.
- Singularity does not optimize for engagement or clicks. It optimizes for **learning outcomes**.

**What we will never do:**

- Sell user data
- Accept paid placement that distorts content
- Allow advertising to influence what knowledge is published or how it is structured
- Put knowledge behind a paywall

## рҹҢҚ Why Singularity Exists

The world has an abundance of knowledge and a crisis of access.

Proprietary platforms lock practical knowledge behind subscriptions. Universities lock it behind tuition. Corporations lock it behind trade secrets. What is freely available is fragmented вҖ” scattered across forums, videos, paywalled journals, and outdated textbooks вҖ” with no unified structure to guide a learner from *not knowing* to *knowing*.

The B.L.U.E. System, and Singularity as its home, exist to fix this. Not to monetize it. Not to own it. To **give it to the world** in a form that actually works.

### The challenges we face (and accept)

Sebastian Rey identified these barriers when developing the B.L.U.E. System. We inherit them вҖ” and commit to fighting them:

| Challenge | Nature | Our Stance |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Corporate legal pressure** | Companies may resist guides that expose proprietary processes | We publish accurate, legal, public-interest knowledge. We fight suppression. |
| **Bureaucratic resistance** | Institutions with interests in knowledge scarcity may push back | Open governance and transparency are our shield. |
| **Content quality at scale** | Maintaining accuracy as contributions grow is genuinely hard | The Verifier System is built to scale with the community. |
| **Funding without compromise** | Staying free while covering real infrastructure costs | Ethical contextual advertising. No exceptions. |
| **Scope of ambition** | "Everything" is a very large project | We build pillar by pillar, level by level, with patience. |

## рҹӨқ How to Contribute

Everyone has something to teach. If you know how to do something вҖ” and you can explain it clearly, step by step вҖ” you can contribute to Singularity.

### Contribution Roles

| Role | What it means |
|------|--------------|
| вңҚпёҸ **Guide Author** | Write a new guide structured by level |
| вң… **Verifier** | Join the jury that reviews guides in your domain |
| вҡ–пёҸ **Auditor** | Resolve content disputes with impartiality |
| рҹ’» **Developer** | Build and improve the platform |
| рҹҢҗ **Translator** | Translate guides to expand global access |
| рҹҗӣ **Reviewer** | Flag inaccurate or outdated content |

### Content Principles

1. **Teach, don't just describe.** A guide must show someone *how* to do something, not just explain *what* it is.
2. **Start at zero.** Every topic begins at Level 1 вҖ” assume no prior knowledge.
3. **Cite everything.** Claims must be sourced. No unverifiable assertions.
4. **No paywalls in the path.** If a guide requires a paid tool with no free alternative, that must be disclosed.
5. **Neutral and accurate.** Guides present how things work вҖ” not opinions about how they should.
